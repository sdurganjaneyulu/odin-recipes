Biryani

Description

Biryani is a rich and aromatic rice dish that is loved across India for its bold flavors and delightful fragrance. Made with long-grain basmati rice, tender pieces of marinated meat, and a blend of traditional spices, biryani is a perfect combination of taste and texture. Each layer of rice and meat is cooked together to absorb the flavors, creating a dish that is both hearty and satisfying

This classic dish is often served on special occasions and family gatherings, but it can also be enjoyed as a comforting meal any day. Garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and served with raita or salad, biryani offers a complete and delicious dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 3–4 green chilies
  • 1/2 cup coriander & mint leaves (combined)
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • Oil or ghee, salt, and water (as needed)
  • 2–3 cloves

Steps

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 20–30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Set some aside for garnishing.
  3. Add whole spices (cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaves) and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Add yogurt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and biryani masala. Mix well.
  7. Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are partially cooked and coated well with the masala.
  8. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves, and mix gently.
  9. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Pour in the required amount of water and add salt to taste.
  10. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors are well combined.
  11. Garnish with the fried onions and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
